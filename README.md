# Sales-Performance-Analysis-Dashboard
This report presents a comprehensive analysis of a sales dataset using Microsoft Excel Pivot Tables and dashboard visualization techniques. The analysis was conducted to answer a series of business questions aimed at understanding transaction behavior, customer demographics, product performance, regional trends, and overall profitability.

The report not only provides quantitative answers but also interprets the results to support data-driven decision-making. Where certain analytical requests could not be fulfilled due to data limitations, such constraints have been explicitly identified and documented.

Dataset Description
The dataset analyzed contains 800 individual sales transactions, capturing key operational variables including:
•	Transaction identifiers and dates
•	Customer demographics (Customer ID, Gender)
•	Product details (Product Name, Product Category, Unit Price, Cost Price)
•	Sales metrics (Quantity Sold, Sales Value)
•	Salesperson and regional information

This broad structure enables multidimensional analysis across time, product, customer, and geography.

Data Cleaning and Preparation
Prior to analysis, the dataset was reviewed to ensure completeness and analytical readiness. No missing values affecting key metrics were identified. To enhance analytical depth, the following calculated fields were introduced:
•	Total Sales/Revenue = Quantity Sold × Unit Price
•	Total Costs = Quantity Sold × Cost Price
•	Profit = Total Sales – Total Costs
•	Profit Margin (%) = (Profit ÷ Total Sales) × 100

These calculated fields formed the basis for revenue and profitability-related pivot analyses.

Key Insights
•	Sales performance is driven by a few high-performing products and regions.
•	Female customers generate higher average transaction values. 
•	Dairy products are the most profitable category.
•	Sales performance peaks in June.
•	Not all analytical questions can be answered without appropriate data structure.

Recommendations
•	Prioritize high-margin and high-revenue products.
•	Strengthen marketing and inventory strategies in high-performing regions.
•	Use salesperson performance metrics for training and incentive programs.
